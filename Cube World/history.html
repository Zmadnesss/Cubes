History

1974
The creation of The Rubik’s Cube…

Ernõ Rubik, a professor from Budapest In Hungary, wanted to help his students understand three-dimensional problems. His solution? The Rubik’s Cube!

His solid cube did things that the world hadn't seen before. It twisted and turned yet it didn’t break. Adding 54 colourful stickers to the six sides gave this amazing puzzle its iconic look. When Ernõ Rubik built his first Cube, it took him over a month to solve it. At that time, he didn’t know that his “Magic Cube” – “Buvos Kocka” in Hungarian - would take the world by storm.

1980
'Magic Cube' is renamed 'Rubik's Cube'

After renaming the cube, it was ready for export over the world. Ideal Toys produces its first batch for exportation and it became popular worldwide.

1982
First annual International Championship

The first Rubik’s Cube world championship takes place in Budapest. Minh Thai, a 16-year-old Vietnamese high school student from Los Angeles, wins the championship by solving the cube in 22.95 seconds.

1995
Diamond Cutters make it expensive

Diamond Cutters International produces the most expensive Rubik’s Cube called the Masterpiece Cube. It is valued at 1.5 million dollars. It is set in 18-karat gold and consists of 22.5 karats of amethyst, 34 karats of rubies, and 34 karats of emeralds.

2017
The ultimate Speed Cube

The best brains at Rubik's team up to create the ultimate Speed Cube.
